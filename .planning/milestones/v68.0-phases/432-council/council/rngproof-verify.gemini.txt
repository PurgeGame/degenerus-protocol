I have completed the independent verification of the RNG-freeze-at-commitment proof for the Degenerus Protocol (frozen tree `4970ba5b`). My analysis focused on the 40 non-trivially-frozen claims and the three specifically flagged cases.

### 1. Adjudication of Flagged Claims

*   **`RNGF-SEAM-RESOLVE` (LOW, proof says `freezeHolds=false`): AGREE.**
    *   **Reasoning:** In `DegenerusGameAdvanceModule.sol:1358-1361`, the gameover path uses a fallback word generated via `_getHistoricalRngFallback(day)`. This function hashes `block.prevrandao` into the result (`:1415`). Since `prevrandao` for a block is only known at the time of its inclusion, and the fallback path is reachable via a permissionless `advanceGame` call after a 14-day VRF stall, an actor can "grind" their `redemptionRoll` by timing their transaction to a block with a favorable `prevrandao`. This allows biasing the outcome magnitude from 25% to 175% of the base ETH value, which violates the freeze invariant.
*   **`RNGF-REDEEMSEAM-08` (HIGH, proof says `freezeHolds=true`): AGREE.**
    *   **Reasoning:** Gambling-burn claims in `sDGNRS.sol` are gated at submission by `game.rngWordForDay(currentPeriod) != 0` (`:1036`), where `currentPeriod` is the current wall-clock day. This pins the commitment to a day whose word is already drawn. The resolution logic (`:916`) then consumes `game.rngWordForDay(day + 1)`. Because `day + 1` is necessarily a future day relative to the submission wall-clock, its VRF word is unknown at commitment.
*   **`RNGF-FLIPESCROW-09` (HIGH, proof says `freezeHolds=true`): AGREE.**
    *   **Reasoning:** Similar to `REDEEMSEAM-08`, the coinflip escrow leg uses `coinflip.getCoinflipDayResult(day + 1)` (`:888`). This result is derived from the future Day D+1 VRF word, which is not drawn or committed until the Day D+1 wall-clock period begins.

### 2. Analysis of Non-Trivial Claims and Refutations

I performed a systematic review of the `CROSS-CONTRACT-SEAM`, `NEEDS-PROOF`, and `MUTABLE-INPUT` claims.

*   **Minor Inconsistency Identified (Labeling Only):** I find that `RNGF-ADV-10` and `RNGF-ADV-12` are marked as `freezeHolds: true` in the index, despite identifying the same `block.prevrandao` dependency as `RNGF-SEAM-RESOLVE`. Strictly following the freeze invariant, these should be classified as `false`. However, as the proof correctly identifies the break in the primary consumer claim (`SEAM-RESOLVE`), this does not change the protocol's risk profile.
*   **Verification of `JKPT-43` (Hero Roll):** I verified that the hero jackpot roll in `JackpotModule.sol:1396` correctly holds its freeze. The weights are read from `dailyHeroWagers[dailyIdx]`, and `dailyIdx` is the protocol's internal day counter which lags the wall-clock day. New bets are written to `dailyHeroWagers[_simulatedDayIndex()]` (the wall-clock day). At the moment the VRF for Day D becomes knowable (Day D+1 wall-clock), all new bets are already routing to the Day D+1 slot, ensuring the Day D wager distribution is frozen.
*   **Verification of `LBX-06` (Boon Roll):** I examined `LootboxModule.sol:1459` and confirmed that while live state (e.g., `deityPassOwners.length`) affects the *weighting* of the roll outcome, the entropy `roll` itself is a frozen bit-slice of the VRF word. The ability to "steer" the outcome by changing global state (like buying a pass before opening a box) is a limited and likely intended game mechanic, not a seed-grinding vulnerability.

### 3. Conclusion

I **CONCUR** with the proof's headline result: **78/79 RNG-consumers are successfully frozen-at-commitment.**

The only identified break is the **gameover prevrandao-fallback magnitude bias** (`RNGF-SEAM-RESOLVE`). This break is correctly classified as **LOW** severity because it is only reachable after a 14-day total VRF failure plus governance inaction, and only affects the redemption multiplier in the terminal protocol state.

I find no additional freeze breaks or actor-controllable biasing paths beyond those already documented.
