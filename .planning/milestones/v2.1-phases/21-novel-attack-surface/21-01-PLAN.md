**Vector 1: Flash Loan + Burn**
- Hypothesis: Attacker flash-loans ETH, inflates sDGNRS reserves, burns DGNRS for inflated share, repays loan.
- Trace the deposit paths: `sDGNRS.receive()` (line ~461, onlyGame modifier), `sDGNRS.depositSteth()` (line ~449, onlyGame modifier). - Show explicit math: if attacker holds X% of supply and sends Y ETH, they recover X% of Y, losing (1-X%)*Y.

**Vector 2: Selfdestruct/Force-Send ETH Injection**
- Hypothesis: Attacker uses CREATE+SELFDESTRUCT (EIP-6780 post-Cancun) to force ETH into sDGNRS, inflating `address(this).balance`.
- Trace: `burn()` at line ~385 reads `ethBal = address(this).balance`. This INCLUDES force-sent ETH.
- Calculate: if attacker holds X% of sDGNRS supply and force-sends Y ETH, they burn to claim X% of the total balance including the Y. Net result: they spent Y to recover X%*Y, losing (1-X%)*Y. This is a DONATION to all holders, not an exploit.
- Must explicitly state: "Force-sent ETH is distributed proportionally to ALL burners, not just the attacker."

**Vector 3: MEV Sandwich on DGNRS Burns**
- Hypothesis: Attacker sees pending DGNRS burn tx in mempool, front-runs with own burn to extract proportionally larger share.
- Trace: Two sequential burns in same block. First burn reduces totalSupply and reserves proportionally. Second burn uses updated (reduced) totalSupply and (reduced) reserves. Since both get proportional shares, front-running a burn does NOT extract extra value -- each gets their fair share.
- Show the math: burner A burns X tokens getting (X/S)*M. Burner B burns Y tokens getting (Y/(S-X))*(M - X/S*M) = (Y/S)*M. The ordering doesn't matter for proportional payouts.

**Vector 4: MEV Sandwich on DGNRS DEX Trades**
- Hypothesis: If DGNRS trades on a DEX, sandwiching trades is standard MEV.
- Analysis: This is standard AMM MEV, not a protocol vulnerability. The protocol itself has no DEX integration. If users choose to list DGNRS on Uniswap, sandwich risk is on the DEX, not the protocol.
- Verdict: OUT_OF_SCOPE (external DEX risk, not protocol vulnerability).

**Vector 5: Burn Arbitrage (market price vs redemption value)**
- Hypothesis: If DGNRS market price < burn redemption value, arbitrageur buys cheap DGNRS and burns for profit.
- Analysis: This is intentional market behavior -- it creates a price floor at redemption value. Not exploitable because it doesn't drain more than the burner's proportional share.
- Show: burn gives exactly proportional share. Buying at discount and burning is just rational arbitrage, not an attack.
