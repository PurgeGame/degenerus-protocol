<threat_model>
## Trust boundary
The internal pool accounting (future/next/current/claimable). The threat is an internal transfer or
consolidation that mints unbacked credit — increasing the total claimable obligation without a matching
real ETH inflow — which would render the protocol insolvent at payout.

## Threat register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation (what the invariant asserts) |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|------------------------------------------|
| T-381-05-01 | Tampering (unbacked credit) | a future->next->current or jackpot/consolidation transfer that increases the total backed obligation without real ETH inflow | mitigate | the ghost conservation ledger: total 4-pool obligation <= cumulative real inflow - cumulative real outflow |
| T-381-05-02 | Information disclosure (insolvency) | the summed pool obligation exceeds ETH+stETH backing | mitigate | assertLe(currentPool+nextPool+futurePool+claimablePool, balance + stETH) every step |
| T-381-05-VAC | (vacuous green) | no consolidation/transfer ever runs, so conservation is trivially preserved | mitigate | a ghost counter of advances/consolidations; the property is meaningful only when transfers actually occur (> 0) |

Severity is blocked on the property being un-fuzzable / vacuously green: if PoolFlowHandler never advances
(no consolidation/skim/jackpot transfer runs) the conservation property is vacuous → the plan FAILS
acceptance until the advance/consolidation counter > 0.
</threat_model>
