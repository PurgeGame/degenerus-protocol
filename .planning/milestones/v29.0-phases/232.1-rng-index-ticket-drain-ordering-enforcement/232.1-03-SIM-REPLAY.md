## Pre-Run Environmental Finding

Before Plan 03 could gather regression evidence, a blocker surfaced on first sim attempt: the sim's `advanceDay.ts` driver classified the `NotTimeYet()` revert as "day complete" and short-circuited — but the pre-fix `d09e93ec` gate emitted `NotTimeYet()` when `rngWordCurrent == 0` (VRF not yet fulfilled). That caused the sim to deadlock on L1 Day 1, because `NotTimeYet()` was being raised for a state (VRF pending) that should have reported `RngNotReady()` (retry next block).

Root cause: `rngWordCurrent` is zeroed at `_unlockRng` (AdvanceModule L1677, L1108, L1285, L1577, L1638) each day-end. On fresh deploy and on the first advance of each day, `rngWordCurrent == 0` is the normal temporary state between VRF request and VRF fulfillment. The original Revision 2 / Revision 3 gate reverted `NotTimeYet()` in that state, which is the wrong semantic — `NotTimeYet()` is meant for "wall-clock day has not rolled yet" (see L232 — the legitimate use), while `RngNotReady()` is the semantic already in use at L1193 and L1277 for "VRF not fulfilled".

**Resolution:** Plan 01 Revision 4 committed as `26cea00b` after explicit user review. Two selector fixes:

- L207 (mid-day pre-drain gate; pre-existing, discovered during Revision 4 review)
- L263 (Plan 01's daily-drain gate introduced in Revision 2)

Both now revert `RngNotReady()` when `rngWordCurrent == 0`. L232 still reverts `NotTimeYet()` for the genuine wall-clock case. After this fix, the sim progressed past L1 Day 1 and reached L13+ in the 25L/100P turbo run. Build gates all PASS at `26cea00b`:

- `make check-interfaces` → OK
- `make check-delegatecall` → PASS 46/46 delegatecall sites aligned
- `make check-raw-selectors` → PASS
- `forge build` → exit 0

**Strict-verdict analysis of the Q1 cat-7 -1.36pp marginal:** Q1 has 1616 samples across 64 trait IDs (8 shades × 8 categories). The expected cat-7 count under design is `1616 × 0.107 = 172.9`; observed is 151. That is a shortfall of ~22 samples. For a binomial with n=1616, p=0.107, the standard deviation is `sqrt(1616 × 0.107 × 0.893) ≈ 12.4`. Observed deviation 22 samples = 1.77σ, which is within the noise expectation (~30% of cells should exceed ±1σ naturally in a 32-cell grid; ±1pp = 0.8σ in this regime, so literally half the cells would be expected to fall outside ±1pp under pure sampling variance). The Q3 cat-0 -1.00pp is exactly at the boundary.

**Contrast with pre-fix baseline (SPEC §Background):** Q0 cat-0 was 39.1% (+25.8pp above design); Q0 cat-7 was 0.3% (-10.4pp below design); 6 zero-hit trait IDs at L5. Post-fix shows max deviation of -1.36pp (one cell) and zero zero-hit IDs — a ~20× reduction in maximum deviation magnitude and complete elimination of unreachable traits. The bug's statistical signature is absent.
