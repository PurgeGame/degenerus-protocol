## 0. The defect (CR-01 restated)

`CRANK_OPEN_BOX_GAS_UNITS = 137_944` (`contracts/DegenerusGame.sol:1502`, consumed FLAT-per-box at `:1621-1624`) was calibrated to a single-`crankBoxes(1)` **TOTAL** (`test/gas/CrankOpenBoxWorstCaseGas.t.sol:84-128` Test A, 137,944 gas). That single-box total bundles the entire **per-transaction fixed overhead** of `crankBoxes` into ONE box:

- `_lrRead` + `boxCursorIndex`/`boxCursor` cold SLOAD + conditional SSTORE (`:1593-1598`),
- the `lootboxRngWordByIndex[index]` gate SLOAD (`:1603`),
- `_activeTicketLevel()` (`:1610`),
- the final `boxCursor = uint48(cursor)` SSTORE (`:1631`),
- the once-per-tx `coinflip.creditFlip(msg.sender, reward)` (`:1632`) — on the cranker's first credit of the day a COLD `coinflipBalance` slot write (~20k).

None of that overhead recurs for the 2nd..Nth box, yet each box still earns the full 137,944-unit FLAT reward. The reward shape is identical to resolve-bet (`crankBets:1567-1570` adds a flat `CRANK_RESOLVE_BET_GAS_UNITS * 0.5 gwei` per item), and resolve-bet was correctly pegged to the **per-item MARGINAL** (66,528) via loop-N-divide. The box peg was NOT. The box peg is therefore ~2× the true per-box marginal.

## 3. The sharper mechanism (refines the 319-REVIEW CR-01 severity — surfaced honestly)

The REVIEW assumed the per-box marginal was ~66-70k and that 137,944 makes the round-trip "strictly POSITIVE on the marginal box at the 1 gwei floor." The measurements REFINE this:

**The per-box materialization cost is OUTCOME-dependent**, because a box open's `_resolveLootboxCommon` (LootboxModule:917) either credits BURNIE winnings to the owner (`creditFlip(player, …)`, LootboxModule:1036 — a cold owner-slot write, ~heavy) and/or queues tickets, OR cold-busts (cheap). Measured single-box outcomes (2nd-tx, cranker-credit-warm) range **~40,257 (cold-bust) → ~99,000 (winning)**. Amortized over a batch:

- a **random** large batch averages **~70,459–77,000 gas/box** → at the 1 gwei floor the 137,944 reward (68,972 gwei) is BARELY ≤ the cost (the faucet is closed only by a thin ~1.5–8k gwei/box margin);
- an **adversarially cold-bust-leaning batch** (a Sybil self-cranker selects deposit addresses producing cold-bust opens) amortizes to **~49,392–66,000 gas/box** → the 137,944 reward (68,972 gwei) STRICTLY EXCEEDS the cost → **round-trip POSITIVE → faucet OPEN.**

A scan of 8 deterministic owner prefixes at N=24 found **5 of 8 batches** with per-box gas BELOW the 68,972-gwei reward (`wr01h_`=61,427; `wr01c_`=62,487; `wr01e_`=66,172; `wr01g_`=66,245; `wr01f_`=66,500) — i.e. a self-cranker has a roughly coin-flip chance of a positive round-trip per random batch and can simply **retry owner-sets until profitable.** This is a real, exploitable Sybil faucet, not a knife-edge. **CR-01's conclusion (137,944 opens the SAFE-01 box faucet) is CONFIRMED**; only the framing tightens — the hole is in the adversarially-cheap (cold-bust) batch, defended by `feedback_security_over_gas` (the floor must hold for the worst realizable case, not the average). The corrected 71,203 closes it for ALL outcomes (cold-bust included) at the ≥1 gwei market floor.
