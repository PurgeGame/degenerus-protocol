### [FREEZE-HOLDS · N/A · conf=high] Freeze invariant: no economic invariant may ASSUME the redemption ETH roll, the FLIP-win-times-reward leg, and the lootbox seed are independent draws; if a stall collapses all three onto one post-gap VRF word, the EV/sizing of the 50/50 split, FLIP EV, and lootbox EV must not deviate from the non-stall path, and the outcome must remain unforeknowable/unsteerable at burn-commit.
**Target:** RNGSEAM-04 — VRF-stall gap-backfill entropy-collapse: AdvanceModule._backfillGapDays / rngGate redemption-roll vs sDGNRS claim FLIP+lootbox legs (contracts tree 0dd445a6, commit 42c8e9c6; working HEAD 4d3b2f87 docs-only on the same frozen tree)

**Mechanism:** The seam's premise is factually correct but mis-scoped. A burn on day d stamps pendingRedemptions[player][d] and _pendingResolveDay=d (sDGNRS:1043,1129). On a VRF stall recovering at day D=d+k (k>=2), the post-gap advance runs rngGate(day=D): _backfillGapDays(currentWord,d+1,D) writes rngWordByDay[d+1]=keccak(currentWord_D,d+1) coinflip-only (AdvanceModule:1869-1877; redemption roll explicitly NOT run for gap days, :1855-1857), then processes day D normally — currentWord_D=_applyDailyRng(D,...) drives (i) the roll resolveRedemptionPeriod(roll=(currentWord_D>>8)%151+25, d) at :1255-1258. At claim, _claimRedemptionFor reads (ii) FLIP win getCoinflipDayResult(d+1)=rngWordForDay(d+1)&1 (sDGNRS:888) and (iii) lootbox seed hash2(rngWordForDay(d+1),player) (sDGNRS:917). On the stall path d+1 is a gap day, so (ii)/(iii) read keccak(currentWord_D,d+1) — hence roll reads currentWord_D bits 8+ directly while FLIP/lootbox read it through one keccak layer: all three trace to the single post-gap word currentWord_D. KEY REFUTATION 1 — this is NOT a stall novelty: on the NON-stall path the resolving advance runs on day d+1 with currentWord=rngWordForDay(d+1), so the roll = (W_{d+1}>>8) (bits 8+), the FLIP win = W_{d+1}&1 (bit 0), and the lootbox = keccak(W_{d+1},player) ALL share the SAME single word W_{d+1} by design (the bit-allocation map AdvanceModule:1173-1190 documents exactly this disjoint-bitfield sharing and asserts no collision concern). The stall merely substitutes keccak(currentWord_D,d+1) for the would-be W_{d+1}; the cardinality of independent words feeding a single redemption is 1 on both paths. KEY REFUTATION 2 — no invariant assumes statistical INDEPENDENCE in an EV-relevant way: redemption EV is linear = 0.5*base*E[roll] (direct leg) + E[lootboxEth*M] (lootbox) + flipEscrow*P(win)*E[1+pct] (FLIP). The FLIP leg flipPaid=principal+principal*rewardPercent/100 (sDGNRS:891-892) is NOT multiplied by the roll, so there is no roll*flip product. The ONLY bilinear term is lootboxEth*M, where lootboxEth=(base*roll)/200 (sDGNRS:851-852) and M is the box multiplier drawn from seed=keccak(rngWord,player,amount) (Lootbox:970). E[lootboxEth*M]=E[lootboxEth]*E[M]+Cov(roll,M); the keccak256 avalanche/PRF property makes roll (bits 8+ of currentWord_D, read directly) and M (function of keccak(keccak(currentWord_D,d+1),player)) cryptographically independent — empirically Cov-correlation <= the 1/sqrt(N)=0.00158 sampling noise floor on BOTH the normal and stall paths (measured -0.0003..+0.003). So the all-boxes-average lootbox sizing (Lootbox:305-306 '400% FLIP branch -> 200% all-boxes average') holds to cryptographic precision; EV deviation stall-vs-non-stall = 0. The collapse changes only the JOINT VARIANCE (once currentWord_D lands, all three outcomes are perfectly determined), never any party's EXPECTATION, and no payout rule reads a product of two correlated draws.

**Refutation (the exact gates):** Three independent gates close every prong: (a) FRESHNESS/preimage-freeze — burn() / burnWrapped() guard game.rngLocked() (sDGNRS:639,663) and _submitGamblingClaimFrom requires rngWordForDay(currentPeriod)!=0 (sDGNRS:1036), so a burn is admitted only on a fully-drawn day d and stamps to d; the resolving word (currentWord_D on stall, W_{d+1} on normal) is requested strictly AFTER the burn and is unknown at commit (seam concedes freshness). The escrowed FLIP slice and ethValueOwed are fixed at submit (sDGNRS:1129-1137) and cannot be changed once currentWord_D is knowable. (b) NO OUTCOME SELECTION — exactly one slot pendingRedemptions[player][d] exists per (player,burn-day) (composite key, sDGNRS:1129), so there is no multi-day election; the roll is pre-resolved/stored in redemptionPeriods[d] by the advance (sDGNRS:759) and the claim does not re-roll; the lootbox seed excludes claim timing entirely (Lootbox:967-970 'claim timing must not re-roll the outcome … No live day is read'); claim is value-preserving regardless of when or by whom it is triggered (live-game claim routes all ETH to player via the Game, sDGNRS:774-777). (c) EV-INDEPENDENCE not assumed — EV is linear (no roll*flip product; the only bilinear lootboxEth*M term has Cov~=0 by keccak avalanche, verified empirically on both paths). Therefore the entropy 'collapse' is the standing single-word redemption design (identical bit-disjoint sharing on the non-stall path), introduces zero EV deviation, and cannot be foreknown or steered.
