### Opportunity 1: Pack redemptionPeriodIndex + redemptionPeriodBurned (save 1 storage slot)

**Current layout:**
- Slot 14: `redemptionPeriodIndex` (uint48, 6 bytes) -- 26 bytes wasted
- Slot 15: `redemptionPeriodBurned` (uint256, 32 bytes)

**Proposed layout:**
- Slot 14: `redemptionPeriodIndex` (uint48, 6 bytes) + `redemptionPeriodBurned` (uint208, 26 bytes)

**Bit-width safety proof:**
- `redemptionPeriodBurned` max value = `totalSupply / 2` = 500,000,000,000 * 1e18 / 2 = 5e29
- uint208 max = 2^208 = ~4.1e62
- 5e29 < 4.1e62: fits with 110+ bits of headroom
- 50% supply cap enforcement at line 686 guarantees this bound

**Co-access pattern:** Both variables are read together in `_submitGamblingClaimFrom` (lines 681-687) and written together when a new period starts (lines 682-684). They are never accessed independently.

**Gas savings (theoretical):**
- Cold path: 1 SSTORE saved (20,000 gas) + 1 SLOAD saved (2,100 gas) = 22,100 gas
- Warm path: 1 SSTORE saved (5,000 gas) + 1 SLOAD saved (100 gas) = 5,100 gas

**Risk:** LOW. Variables always co-accessed. uint208 has 110+ bits of headroom.

**Code change:**
```solidity
// BEFORE (2 slots):
uint48  internal redemptionPeriodIndex;    // slot 14

// AFTER (1 slot):

```

(Same structure)

**Bit-width safety proof:**
- `ethValueOwed` per user: bounded by user's share of total ETH. Max = total ETH pool = ~1e23 wei (87 bits). uint128 fits.
- `burnieOwed` per user: bounded by user's share of total BURNIE. Max = total BURNIE supply = ~1e30 (100 bits). uint128 fits.
