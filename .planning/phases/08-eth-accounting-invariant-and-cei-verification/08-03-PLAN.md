  <name>Task 1: Verify DegenerusVault share redemption rounding (ACCT-06)</name>
  <files>contracts/DegenerusVault.sol</files>
  <action>
    Read DegenerusVault.sol in full. Focus on:

    Step 1 — Read the deposit and redemption functions:
    - Locate the `deposit()` function: how are shares calculated from ETH deposit? What is the share issuance formula?
    - Locate the `withdraw()` (or `redeem()`) function: find the `claimAmount = (reserveBalance * sharesBurned) / totalSupply` line
    - Note the exact line numbers and variable names

    Step 2 — Verify rounding direction:
    - Solidity integer division floors (rounds toward zero). For positive operands this rounds DOWN.
    - claimAmount rounded DOWN means claimer receives LESS than their exact proportional share
    - This is CORRECT direction for solvency (protocol retains fractional rounding remainder)
    - Verify: is there any division in the DEPOSIT formula that could round UP, potentially inflating shares issued vs. ETH received?

    Step 3 — Test the partial-burn extraction scenario:
    - Consider: player deposits X ETH → receives N shares. Player burns N/2 shares → receives floor(balance * N/2 / N) ETH. Remaining N/2 shares. Burns remaining N/2 shares → receives floor(new_balance * N/2 / N/2) ETH.
    - Does the two-step partial-burn yield more ETH than a single full burn? Due to floor rounding, a single full burn always gives ≥ two partial burns. Verify this algebraically.
    - The two-burn sequence: round 1 gets floor(B * 0.5) = B/2 - ε₁. Round 2 gets floor((B - B/2 + ε₁) * 1.0) = B/2 + ε₁. Total = B - ε₁ + ε₁ = B. No extraction; rounding favor alternates.
    - Confirm or refute this with the actual formula.

    Step 4 — Read the total-burn / 1T refill mechanism:
    - Locate the code that handles totalSupply reaching zero or a threshold
    - Does the refill create a window where the next depositor's shares are disproportionately large relative to existing holders?
    - Is the refill atomic with the last withdrawal, or is there a multi-block race condition?

    Step 5 — DegenerusVault receive() (ACCT-06 / VAULT-01 adjacent):
    - Read whether DegenerusVault has a `receive()` function
    - If it does: does a direct ETH send inflate `reserveBalance` without issuing shares, benefiting existing holders?
    - Note this separately from ACCT-06 (VAULT-01 covers it in Phase 11 — flag as out-of-scope here but note if found)

    Produce ACCT-06 verdict: PASS or FINDING-SEVERITY with:
    - Rounding direction: safe or exploitable
    - Partial-burn sequence: yields no surplus or yields surplus (with amount)
    - Refill mechanism: atomic or raceable
