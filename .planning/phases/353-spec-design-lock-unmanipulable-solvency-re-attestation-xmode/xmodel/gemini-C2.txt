VERDICT: NEEDS-DESIGN-CHANGE
RATIONALE: The system correctly prevents settle-time manipulation by strictly fixing all seed inputs (`windowBoundaryDay`, `sender`, `storedCode`) at the moment of subscription. However, because the seed is entirely deterministic and known in advance, it creates two major structural exploits for a colluding buyer and affiliate. First, it enables a "free option" path-arbitrage: the attacker calculates the scheduled roll outcome in advance; if the affiliate wins, they wait for the scheduled flush (100% payout), but if higher uplines win, the buyer triggers an early player-flush to override the roll with the deterministic 75% split. Second, a self-referring buyer can perform "vanity-grinding" before subscribing by generating multiple sybil affiliate addresses (`storedCode`s) until they find one that guarantees the scheduled roll lands on their affiliate (0-14). The "buyer-never-wins" guard is ineffective here because it only blocks the exact buyer address (`sender`), not a sybil affiliate address. Both vectors allow an attacker to systematically steal yield intended for higher uplines.
SEED-GRIND-VECTOR: 1) Path-Arbitrage Option: The buyer checks the known future seed; if it pays upline1/2, the buyer triggers an early player-flush to force the 75% deterministic split. 2) Sybil Code Grinding: Before subscribing, the buyer generates a sybil affiliate address that produces a winning `keccak256` hash for their specific `sender` and `windowBoundaryDay`, guaranteeing a 100% scheduled payout.
