I found a churn edge in the design as stated: the deterministic player-flush must explicitly inherit `buyer-never-wins` per split leg. Otherwise referral cycles turn the 20% upline leg into a direct buyer payout.

Current affiliate semantics prevent direct self-referral, but they do not prevent a 2-wallet cycle: A is referred by B, B is referred by A. For A’s buy, the chain is affiliate=B, upline1=A, upline2=B. In the scheduled WTA path, the 20% upline1 outcome is skipped because winner == sender. In a literal deterministic 75/20/5 player flush, A gets that 20% slice.

Arithmetic with one day at max `dailyQuantity=255`, fresh level >=4, no kickback, untapered: affiliate base is about `255 * 200 = 51,000 BURNIE`. Steady scheduled EV to attacker-controlled wallets is `80% * 51,000 = 40,800` because the 20% buyer outcome skips. Churn deterministic payout is `100% * 51,000 = 51,000`. Gross churn gain is `10,200 BURNIE` per cycle before gas. At 500k gas and 20 gwei, cost is `0.01 ETH`; break-even is `0.01 / 10,200 = 0.00000098 ETH/BURNIE`, or `0.00098 ETH per 1,000 BURNIE`. Above that implied value, the loop is positive EV. At 10 days, gross gain scales to `102,000 BURNIE`, so gas is not a meaningful bound.

The other proposed vectors do not work if the deterministic buyer-skip is fixed: taper is per-buy and immutable, the accumulator/`lastSettledDay` prevents double-settle, the clamp only under-credits, and early settlement only accelerates nominally equal BURNIE unless time value exceeds gas. I would also lock that deterministic split does not call affiliate quest credit independently per leg in a way that mints more fixed quest rewards than the WTA-equivalent path.
