I have completed the audit of the **RNG-FREEZE spine** for the Degenerus Protocol. My investigation focused on the freshness of RNG consumers, the distribution of narrowed seeds, and the integrity of the freeze-window across all changed modules.

### Audit Findings Summary

#### 1. (RNG-01) Freshness & Commitment Points
**VERIFIED SOUND.**
Every RNG consumer on the changed surface descends from a word committed **before** the player's input or is keyed to an undrawn future word.
- **Degenerette:** Bets are bound to a `lootboxRngIndex` and a unique `betId` (nonce) at placement (`_placeDegeneretteBetCore`). The placement reverts if the RNG word for that index is already on-chain (`lootboxRngWordByIndex[index] != 0`), ensuring the outcome is unknown at commitment.
- **Decimator:** Winners are selected from the full VRF word at the moment of level resolution (`DecimatorModule:269`). Claim-time lootboxes use a narrowed 32-bit word, but the player's commitment (the burn) occurred during the level, long before the resolution word was drawn.
- **Redemption:** Burns are admitted only if the current wall-clock day's word is already on-chain (`rngWordForDay(currentPeriod) != 0`). The lootbox leg reads `day + 1`, which is guaranteed to be zero at the time of burn since `advanceGame` never draws future words.

#### 2. (RNG-02) Decimator `uint32` Claim-Seed Distribution
**VERIFIED SOUND.**
The narrowing of the claim-time lootbox seed to 32 bits (`round.rngWord = uint32(rngWord)`) is sound and unbiased.
- **Distribution:** While the 32-bit floor limits the unique seeds to ~4 billion, the per-player address mixing (`hash2(word, address)`) ensures that outcomes are independent and uniform across the winning population. Keccak256 provides full diffusion, so sharing a 32-bit component does not correlate the results of distinct addresses.
- **Grinding:** Since the word is drawn via VRF *after* players have committed their addresses (via burns), they cannot steer the result. The permissionless nature of claims removes any timing-based advantage for the winner.

#### 3. (RNG-03) One-Shot / Replay Safety
**VERIFIED SOUND.**
All permissionless resolution paths are single-shot by construction.
- **Record Clearing:** `LootboxModule.openBox` zeroes `lootboxEth[index][player]` before resolution (`:579`). `DegeneretteModule._resolveFullTicketBet` deletes the bet before processing spins (`:646`). `DecimatorModule._claimDecimatorJackpotFor` marks the entry as claimed (`e.claimed = 1`) before awarding lootboxes (`:398`).
- **Replay Prevention:** A transaction revert to "observe" an outcome will also revert the state change (restoring the bet/box), but since the seed is fixed, the same outcome will occur upon any retry. The `address(this) == GAME` guard in box-spin resolvers ensures they execute only within the intended delegatecall context.

#### 4. (RNG-04) Domain Separation
**VERIFIED SOUND.**
Despite dropping the `amount` term in `resolveLootboxDirect`, caller-side domain separation is airtight.
- **Degenerette:** Supplies `hash2(rngWord, betId)`, where `betId` is a unique per-player nonce.
- **Decimator:** Supplies the narrowed `round.rngWord`, and `resolveLootboxDirect` mixes it with the unique `player` address.
- **Redemption:** Supplies `hash2(day+1Word, player)` and uses a `hash1` rehash chain to separate the 5 resolution chunks.

#### 5. (RNG-05) Redemption `day+1` Pre-Draw Gate
**VERIFIED SOUND.**
The gate `game.rngWordForDay(currentPeriod) != 0` ensures the burn is committed only after the current day is settled. Because `dailyIdx` is advanced monotonically and never exceeds the current wall-clock day, `rngWordForDay(currentPeriod + 1)` is always zero at the time of burn, preventing any known-draw grinding.

#### 6. (RNG-06) SLOAD Freeze-Invariance
**VERIFIED SOUND.**
All critical inputs to RNG-derived outcomes are frozen snapshots.
- **Activity Score:** Read from the frozen lootbox record, packed bet, or burn bucket. Never a live read inside the resolution window.
- **Boon Interpretation:** While some boon interpretation reads (like `level` or `decWindowOpen`) are live, they only afford a "timing edge" where a player can choose when to resolve an already-fixed outcome. This is a documented, by-design behavior.
- **EntropyLib:** `hash1` and `hash2` migrations are byte-identical to `keccak256(abi.encode(...))` for full-width uint256 words.
