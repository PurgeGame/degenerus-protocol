<objective>
Model the affiliate referral system's extraction potential. The critical insight from research is that affiliate rewards are denominated in BURNIE tokens (minted via creditFlip/creditCoin), not ETH. This means affiliate rewards do not drain the prize pool. This plan verifies that claim, models circular referral structures, analyzes the deterministic winner roll for exploitation, and determines whether any referrer+referee combination can extract more total value than deposited.

Purpose: ECON-03 requires proving that the affiliate referral system does not create positive-sum extraction. Since rewards are BURNIE (inflationary mint), the question becomes whether BURNIE rewards have enough ETH-equivalent value to make circular referral profitable after accounting for the ETH cost of generating referral volume.

**2. Circular Referral Model**

Model a pair: Player A refers Player B, Player B refers Player A.

Scenario: Both players buy 1 ETH of tickets at level 1 (fresh ETH, 25% affiliate rate).

For each player's purchase:
a. Player A buys 1 ETH of tickets:
   - 1 ETH goes to prize pool (nextPrizePool/futurePrizePool split)
   - Player B receives affiliate reward: 25% of 1 ETH = 0.25 ETH worth of BURNIE
   - Rakeback to Player A: 0-25% of the affiliate amount (0 to 0.0625 ETH worth of BURNIE)

b. Player B buys 1 ETH of tickets:
   - Same pattern, Player A receives affiliate reward

c. Combined ETH deposited: 2 ETH
d. Combined BURNIE received: ~0.5 ETH worth of BURNIE (minus rakeback adjustments)
e. Combined ETH in prize pool: 2 ETH (full amount)

**Key question:** Is the BURNIE worth 0.5 ETH? If BURNIE can be converted to ETH at 1:1, then the pair gets back 0.5 ETH (25%) as BURNIE while depositing 2 ETH. Net loss = 1.5 ETH in ETH terms. If BURNIE is worth less than ETH, the loss is greater.

**3. Weighted Winner Roll Analysis**

Read `_rollWeightedAffiliateWinner` (lines 891-923):
a. The entropy uses `keccak256(AFFILIATE_ROLL_TAG, currentDay, sender, storedCode)`. This is:
   - Deterministic given (day, sender, code)
   - Computable off-chain before the transaction
   - Changes daily (new day = new entropy)
b. Can an attacker precompute which day gives them the favorable roll?
   - For a 2-party system (only direct + rakeback), there is only 1 recipient. Winner roll is trivial.
   - For a 3-tier system with 3 recipients, the winner gets the combined payout.
c. Does day-alignment matter? The roll selects one recipient per transaction. Over N transactions, each recipient gets their proportional share in expectation. The day-alignment could give a +/- 1 transaction variance but does not change the long-term EV.
d. Quantify: what is the maximum EV advantage from optimal day-alignment vs random timing? (Expected: negligible -- less than gas cost.)

**4. Fresh vs Recycled ETH Rates**

a. When does ETH count as "fresh" vs "recycled"? Expected: recycled = claimable winnings used for purchase, fresh = new msg.value.
b. Recycled rate is 5% (vs 20-25% fresh). Can an attacker force all purchases to be "fresh" by never recycling?
c. Does the fresh/recycled distinction affect the extraction model significantly?

**5. Affiliate Bonus Points Interaction**

a. Affiliate rewards also contribute to activity score (affiliate bonus, max 50 points / 5,000 BPS).
b. Circular referrals generate affiliate bonus points for both parties.
c. Does this create a secondary value channel? If both A and B earn affiliate bonus points, their lootbox EV increases.
d. Quantify: the marginal lootbox EV benefit from affiliate bonus points earned through circular referrals. Is this the real extraction vector?

**6. BURNIE Value Model**

a. How can BURNIE be converted back to ETH? Options: DGNRS exchange, coinflip (50% win rate = 50% expected return), direct market.
b. Coinflip: BURNIE -> bet -> 50% chance of 2x payout in BURNIE. Net EV = 100% (break even) in BURNIE terms.
c. Is there any mechanism to redeem BURNIE for ETH directly? If not, BURNIE value depends on external market.
d. For the purpose of ECON-03: if BURNIE has zero ETH value, then affiliate extraction is zero. If BURNIE has nonzero value, the extraction is bounded by BURNIE/ETH rate.

**7. ECON-03 Verdict**

Synthesize:
a. Affiliate rewards are BURNIE, not ETH. They do not drain the prize pool.
b. Circular referrals generate BURNIE but cost ETH. Net extraction depends on BURNIE/ETH rate.
c. Even at 1:1 BURNIE/ETH, the referrer+referee pair deposits 2 ETH and receives ~0.5 ETH worth of BURNIE. They lose 1.5 ETH net.
d. The weighted winner roll is deterministic but EV-neutral over multiple transactions.
e. The real extraction from circular referrals is the affiliate bonus points (activity score), which is bounded and analyzed in 05-02.

ECON-03 Verdict: PASS/FAIL with complete reasoning.
