READ-ONLY audit. Do NOT modify any contract files.

Build a mathematical model of Sybil group returns for each prize distribution channel. Define a Sybil group G with N accounts that collectively own fraction F of total tickets at a given level, with total group deposit D.

**1. Prize Pool Funding Analysis**

Establish the zero-sum baseline:
- Total prize pool P at a level = sum of all player deposits at that level (minus stETH yield, which is protocol surplus)
- Group G deposits D = F * P (they own fraction F of the pool they funded)
- Total payout from all channels = P (minus any protocol retention)
- For G to have positive EV, they need expected group payout > D

**2. Scatter Jackpot Channel (50% of pool)**

Read `contracts/modules/DegenerusGameAdvanceModule.sol` to trace scatter jackpot distribution:
a. How are scatter winners selected? Is it proportional to ticket count, or trait-weighted?
b. If trait-weighted: how do traits map to tickets? Does owning more tickets give more traits?
c. Key: with 256 possible traits and VRF-based selection, does owning F% of tickets give approximately F% of scatter wins?
d. Model: E[scatter_payout_G] = f(F, trait_distribution). Is this proportional, sub-proportional, or super-proportional to F?

**3. Daily Jackpot Channel (6-14% of pool)**

a. What gates daily jackpot eligibility? (Expected: 8-mint streak requirement)
b. Among eligible players, how is the winner selected?
c. Key: a Sybil group splitting deposits means each account has fewer mints. Does this reduce streak eligibility?
d. Model: If group splits D across N accounts, each account has D/N mints. If D/N < streak requirement, some accounts are ineligible.
e. Compare: 1 account with D mints (always eligible) vs N accounts with D/N mints (some ineligible).

**4. BAF Channel (10% of pool)**

a. How is BAF distributed? (Expected: coinflip-weighted)
b. Does owning more tickets affect BAF weight?
c. Model: E[BAF_payout_G] as function of F.

**5. Affiliate Draw Channel (11.25% of pool)**

a. How is affiliate draw winner selected? (Expected: affiliate-score-weighted)
b. A Sybil group self-referring creates circular referrals. Does this increase or decrease net EV? (Research says affiliate rewards are BURNIE, not ETH, so they don't drain the ETH prize pool.)
c. Model: E[affiliate_draw_G] as function of F.

**6. Lootbox Channel**

a. Lootbox EV is activity-score-dependent. A Sybil group splitting deposits dilutes per-account activity scores (each account has lower streak, quest participation, mint count).
b. Key: 1 account with full activity score gets 135% EV on up to 10 ETH. N accounts each get lower EV (due to diluted scores) on 10 ETH each (but total cap = N * 10 ETH).
c. Model: Is N * (10 ETH * lower_multiplier) > 1 * (10 ETH * 1.35)? Under what conditions?

**7. Composite Model**

Sum per-channel expected payouts for group G:
E[total_G] = E[scatter_G] + E[daily_G] + E[BAF_G] + E[affiliate_G] + E[lootbox_G] + E[decimator_G]

Compare to group deposit D.

**8. Activity Score Dilution Analysis**

For a player with total ETH D choosing between:
- Strategy A: 1 account, full D deposited (max activity score)
- Strategy B: N accounts, D/N each (diluted activity scores)

Compute the net EV difference for each strategy at D = 10 ETH, 50 ETH, 100 ETH.

**9. ECON-01 Verdict**

Is E[total_G] / D > 1 for any F or N? If proportional across all channels, the answer is no (zero-sum minus protocol fees). If any channel is super-proportional, document the conditions.

Write the complete findings document with:
- Sections 1-6: Per-channel analysis
- Section 7: Composite model
- Section 8: Activity score dilution
- Section 9: ECON-01 verdict (PASS/FAIL with complete reasoning)
- Section 10: Findings (any issues rated by severity)
