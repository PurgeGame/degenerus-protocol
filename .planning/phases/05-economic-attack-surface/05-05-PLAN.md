<objective>
Analyze whether a block proposer (validator) can manipulate advanceGame timing to control which level transitions occur and influence outcomes. The validator threat model is the strongest realistic attacker for an on-chain game. They can choose transaction ordering and inclusion within their block but cannot modify the VRF word or change the deterministic game state.

Purpose: ECON-05 requires proving that a block proposer cannot manipulate advanceGame timing to control level transitions. The key is that VRF words are pre-committed, day-index gates limit frequency, and outcomes are deterministic from (VRF word, game state).

Output: `05-05-FINDINGS-block-proposer-timing.md` containing the timing manipulation analysis and ECON-05 verdict.

**2. Block Proposer Timing Control**

Model what a block proposer can and cannot do:

a. **Include/exclude advanceGame:** The proposer can exclude advanceGame from their block. This delays it by 12 seconds (next block). But any user can submit advanceGame, and the NEXT proposer may include it. A proposer cannot permanently block advanceGame -- only delay by one block.

b. **Timestamp manipulation:** The proposer can adjust block.timestamp within the consensus-allowed window (typically +-15 seconds from true time). Since `day = block.timestamp / DAY_LENGTH`, a +-15 second manipulation can:
   - At a day boundary: potentially include advanceGame one block earlier or later
   - Within a day: no effect (day value is the same)
   - Compute: for DAY_LENGTH = 86400 (24 hours), a 15-second manipulation at the boundary changes nothing meaningful. For DAY_LENGTH = 1 day, the precision is already 1 day.

c. **Transaction ordering:** The proposer can place their own transactions before or after advanceGame. But since advanceGame's outputs are deterministic from (rngWordCurrent, game state), ordering only matters for:
   - Buying tickets before vs after a level transition (analyzed in 05-04)
   - Claiming winnings before vs after jackpot distribution

**3. VRF Word Commitment Analysis**

a. The VRF word is generated by Chainlink and delivered via rawFulfillRandomWords.
b. The proposer who includes rawFulfillRandomWords in their block can see the VRF word. BUT:
   - By Phase 2: rngLockedFlag blocks state-changing operations during VRF processing
   - The proposer cannot both fulfill VRF AND call advanceGame in the same block (rngLockedFlag prevents it)
   - Wait -- verify this. Is rngLockedFlag cleared by rawFulfillRandomWords itself? If so, the proposer COULD call advanceGame in the same block as VRF fulfillment.
c. Trace the exact sequence:
   - rawFulfillRandomWords stores rngWordCurrent and clears rngLockedFlag (or does it?)
   - If rngLockedFlag is cleared in the same tx, then advanceGame is callable in the next block
   - Even if callable in the same block: advanceGame's outcome is deterministic from the now-stored VRF word. The proposer sees the VRF word but cannot change the game's response to it.

**4. Proposer Profit Scenarios**

a. **Scenario 1: Delay advanceGame to buy more tickets at current level price**
   - Proposer excludes advanceGame, buys tickets at current (lower) price.
   - Next block: advanceGame transitions to higher price level.
   - Profit: proposer bought cheap tickets. But these tickets are in the current level's pool, not the next level's. No cross-level value extraction.

b. **Scenario 2: Delay advanceGame to prevent a rival from winning jackpot**
   - Proposer delays by one block. But the jackpot outcome is deterministic from VRF word.
   - Delaying does not change who wins -- just when the win is processed.

c. **Scenario 3: Include advanceGame + own purchase in same block**
   - Proposer includes advanceGame first (transitions level), then buys tickets at new price.
   - No profit: they pay the correct new price.

d. **Scenario 4: Withhold rawFulfillRandomWords**
   - Proposer sees VRF fulfillment and decides not to include it (if the VRF word would cause an unfavorable outcome).
   - BUT: VRF fulfillment comes from Chainlink, not user transactions. The coordinator sends it to rawFulfillRandomWords. Can the proposer censor it?
   - Yes, a proposer CAN exclude any transaction from their block. But the VRF fulfillment would be included by the next honest proposer.
   - 18-hour timeout: if VRF is not fulfilled, the game can re-request after 18 hours.
   - Proposer cannot control the VRF word -- only delay its inclusion.

**5. ECON-05 Verdict**

Synthesize:
- The proposer's only lever is WHEN, not WHAT.
- VRF words are committed by Chainlink, not the proposer.
- advanceGame outcomes are deterministic from (VRF word, game state).
- block.timestamp manipulation has no meaningful effect (day-level granularity).
- Jackpot winner selection is VRF-derived, not block-derivable.
- Delaying does not change outcomes, only timing.

ECON-05 Verdict: PASS/FAIL with specific analysis per scenario.
