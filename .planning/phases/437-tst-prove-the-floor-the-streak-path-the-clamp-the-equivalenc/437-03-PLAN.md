<objective>
Prove TST-03: the point-domain consumer math reproduces the intended pre-change (bps-domain) outcomes for the three activity-score consumers — the Lootbox EV multiplier, the Degenerette ROI (+ WWXRP high ROI), and the Decimator multiplier + bucket — across the TABLE-A threshold anchors and a whole-point grid.

Purpose: the v69 IMPL migrated every consumer threshold from bps to points. Two pre-conditions (the floored on-chain score is always a clean whole point, and every TABLE-A anchor converts by exact ÷100) make the comparisons and ratios scale-invariant for clean scores — EXCEPT the one non-scale-invariant migration: the Decimator multiplier `bonusBps/3` re-expressed as `(points*100)/3`. This plan builds a point-domain oracle and a bps-domain oracle and asserts bit-identity cell-by-cell on the whole-point grid, with special focus on the Decimator re-scale (a naive `points/3` would be ~100x wrong).

Output: new `test/fuzz/ConsumerPointEquivalence.t.sol`. Committed autonomously (no `contracts/*.sol` change).
