This is a textbook blind spot in the property net. The fuzz net asserts boundary ceilings and global conservation but fails to assert the structural integrity of the liabilities generating the payouts, nor the liveness of the state transitions.

Here is the exact finding.

### 1. The MISSING Invariant: Liability Integrity / Ticket Conservation (Solvency Class)
**Unstated Property:** `Total Valid Tickets (or Hero Wagers) == Total Tickets Paid For`.
None of FUZZ-01..05 checks the *validity or conservation of the accounting mechanisms* that distribute the jackpot pools. `POOL-CONSERVATION` ensures ETH doesn't vanish or spawn from nowhere, and `SOLVENCY` ensures the internal ledger matches the `claimablePool`. However, neither asserts that the shares dictating the payout (Tickets, Hero Wagers, or Afking sub buckets) were legitimately minted and backed by proportional ETH.

### 2. The Reachable Action Sequence
A malicious user can extract the jackpot without violating the 5 asserted invariants by manipulating the **Hero Wager / Ticket Accounting**:
1. **The Setup:** The attacker waits for the VRF window to open (or immediately precedes it) on a `lastPurchaseDay` (Ticket Jackpot day).
2. **The Exploit:** The attacker calls `placeDegeneretteBet()` using batch ticket creation or zero-cost Afking subscription entries that bypass the strict ETH-to-ticket accounting checks, artificially inflating their `dailyHeroWagers` or ticket balances.
3. **The Blind Spot:** Because no ETH is actually added or removed from the game's global balance, `POOL-CONSERVATION` holds perfectly. 
4. **The Payout:** When `advanceGame()` resolves the jackpot via `_rollHeroSymbol` (reading the manipulated `dailyHeroWagers`), the attacker captures the vast majority of the jackpot. The ETH moves from `Next/Future` pools to `claimablePool`, and their packed balance increases proportionally. `SOLVENCY` holds perfectly because the sum of balances still matches `claimablePool`. 
5. **Result:** The attacker successfully steals the jackpot from legitimate players while the fuzz net remains 100% green.

### Alternative Missing Invariant: Liveness via Unbounded Revert (Gas-DoS Class)
**Unstated Property:** `advanceGame() succeeds (does not revert)`.
FUZZ-03 asserts `advanceGame` *consumes <= 16.7M gas*. A reverting transaction consumes very little gas, trivially satisfying FUZZ-03 while permanently bricking the protocol.

**Violating Sequence:**
1. A Chainlink VRF stall occurs (or is maliciously forced via fee exhaustion).
2. An actor repeatedly calls `retryLootboxRng()` every 6 hours (`MIDDAY_RNG_RETRY_TIMEOUT`), which continuously increments the reserved `LR_INDEX_SHIFT` without filling the RNG words.
3. Over an extended stall, this creates a massive gap of hundreds of orphaned indices.
4. When the VRF word finally lands, `advanceGame()` calls `_backfillOrphanedLootboxIndices(vrfWord)`. This function executes an **unbounded backward scan** `for (uint48 i = idx - 1; i >= 1; )` attempting to hash and fill every orphaned slot.
5. The loop hits the block gas limit and reverts. `advanceGame()` is permanently bricked. The protocol enters a zombie state, but FUZZ-03 passes because the reverted call cost < 16.7M gas.

**Severity:** **CRITICAL**. Both gaps represent protocol-ending threats (Total Fund Theft and Permanent Bricking) that seamlessly bypass the current invariant net. 

To achieve completeness, the net MUST add a **Liability Conservation** property tracking `Ticket/Hero Supply vs Paid ETH`, and a **Liveness** gate asserting that `advanceGame` resolves without reverting from fuzzed pre-states.
