## Per-Phase Summary

### Phase 50: Skim Redesign Audit -- SAFE

3 findings (0 MEDIUM, 3 INFO). All 10 requirements passed.

The 5-step futurepool skim pipeline is arithmetically correct: overshoot surcharge is monotonic (f'(x) = 40,000,000 / (x+10000)^2 > 0) and capped at 3500 bps, ratio adjustment is bounded at +/-400 bps with underflow prevention via `penalty >= bps ? 0 : bps - penalty`, bit-field consumption has documented overlap (F-50-01, F-50-02 -- INFO, functionally independent via modulo), triangular variance cannot underflow take (halfWidth clamp at line 1033 guarantees subtraction safety), take cap at 80% holds as hard clamp post-variance, ETH conservation proven algebraically (T and I cancel exactly in the three state updates), and insurance skim is exact `floor(N/100)` above 100 wei (sub-100 unreachable from 50 ETH bootstrap). Economic behavior verified: overshoot accelerates growth as designed (R=3.0 -> +2545 bps), stall escalation functions independently of removed growth adjustment (+100 bps/week), and level 1 is safe with lastPool=50 ETH bootstrap (F-50-03 test gap).

### Phase 51: Redemption Lootbox Audit -- SAFE (1 MEDIUM)

3 findings (1 MEDIUM, 2 INFO). All 7 requirements passed.

The 50/50 split correctly routes ETH with algebraic conservation (`ethDirect + lootboxEth == totalRolledEth` for all inputs; extra wei on odd totals goes to lootbox). gameOver burns bypass lootbox entirely (100% direct ETH, no lootbox call -- intentional design, favorable to player). 160 ETH daily cap is enforced via cumulative `uint256` comparison before `uint96` cast, with period gating preventing cross-period stacking. Slot packing is correct (96+96+48+16 = 256 bits exactly, all cast sites verified safe under economic constraints). Activity score snapshot is immutable from write through consumption across 3 contracts (write-once guard, local capture before delete, pass-through forwarding). Cross-contract access control is valid (compile-time `ContractAddresses.SDGNRS` check, delegatecall preserves context). REDM-06-A is a MEDIUM accounting corruption (unchecked subtraction in `resolveRedemptionLootbox`) that cannot be exploited for direct theft because `claimablePool -= payout` reverts on the inflated amount.

### Phase 52: Invariant Test Suite -- PASS

- **INV-03:** Redemption split conservation at pure arithmetic level (3 tests, 3000 fuzz runs) and lifecycle level via INV-08 (256 runs x 128 depth)

All 26 FuturepoolSkimTest and 11 RedemptionInvariants pass with zero regressions.
