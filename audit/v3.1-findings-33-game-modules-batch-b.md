### CMT-027: _processAutoRebuy NatSpec says "next level or next+1 (50/50)" but code uses 1-4 levels (25% each)

- **What:** The `@dev` NatSpec for `_processAutoRebuy` at line 980 states `Converts winnings to tickets for next level or next+1 (50/50)`. The actual implementation calls `_calcAutoRebuy` (PayoutUtils line 54-58) which computes `levelOffset = (entropy & 3) + 1`, producing values 1 through 4 with 25% probability each. Level offset 1 targets `currentLevel + 1` (next level, 25% chance), while offsets 2-4 target `currentLevel + 2/3/4` (75% chance, routed to futurePrizePool). The "50/50" split and "next or next+1" range are both wrong.
- **Where:** DegenerusGameJackpotModule.sol:980
- **Why:** A warden modeling auto-rebuy fund flow would assume a 50/50 split between the next two levels, significantly underestimating the spread across 4 levels and the 75% probability of funds going to futurePrizePool vs. 25% to nextPrizePool. This matters for prize pool sustainability analysis: the actual design channels 75% of auto-rebuy ETH into the future pool (long-term reserve) vs. only 25% into the immediate next level, which is a deliberately conservative approach that the NatSpec fails to communicate.
- **Suggestion:** Change to: `Converts winnings to tickets 1-4 levels ahead (25% each; +1 goes to nextPrizePool, +2/+3/+4 go to futurePrizePool).`
- **Category:** comment-inaccuracy
- **Severity:** INFO

### CMT-031: recordDecBurn NatSpec says "player burn resets" but burn carries over on bucket migration

- **What:** The `@dev` NatSpec for `recordDecBurn` at line 213 states `On improvement, previous burn is removed from old aggregate, player burn resets, and entry migrates.` In the actual code (lines 239-248), when a better bucket is selected: (1) the old subbucket aggregate is decremented by `prevBurn` -- correct; (2) the new bucket and subbucket are assigned -- correct; (3) the new subbucket is seeded with the carried-over `prevBurn` value (line 246: `_decUpdateSubbucket(lvl, m.bucket, m.subBucket, prevBurn)`). The player's `m.burn` field is never zeroed -- it retains `prevBurn` and subsequent burns accumulate from there. The burn does not reset; it migrates.
- **Where:** DegenerusGameDecimatorModule.sol:213
- **Why:** A warden reading "player burn resets" would expect the player's burn counter to restart from zero after switching to a better bucket, which would mean the multiplier cap (`DECIMATOR_MULTIPLIER_CAP = 200 * PRICE_COIN_UNIT`) resets and the player gets a fresh multiplier allowance. In reality, the burn carries over -- the player's accumulated burn history (and therefore remaining multiplier cap headroom) is preserved. This distinction matters for modeling the maximum effective burn a player can achieve across bucket switches.
- **Suggestion:** Change to: `On improvement, previous burn is removed from old aggregate, entry migrates to new bucket with burn carried over.`
- **Category:** comment-inaccuracy
- **Severity:** INFO
